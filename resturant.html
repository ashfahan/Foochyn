Critic Rating
Mini Ribeiro Speaks
Food critic, features writer and columnist

This all day dining place serves delectable buffets for breakfast, lunch and dinner apart from an À la carte menu which offers Indian and global cuisines. The seating arrangement is simple but well-spaced and the poolside view adds to the charm. The live counters which change daily are interactive and a treat to watch. The lunch and dinner buffets are an exercise in choices with abundant options for vegetarians too. Akhrot Aur Zimikand Ki Seekh and Dahi Batata Puri are worth trying for vegetarians. Lamb Paya Shorba, Prawns Tempura, Tawa Meat Masala are treats carnivores must not miss. The quality of ingredients is incomparable and the flavours robust. Affordably priced, the buffets here are hugely popular. Those seeking a quick bite can opt for the sandwiches, burgers and rolls section. The bar menu is exhaustive and the signature cocktails are interesting. The Indian desserts here are definitely your best bet. The mawa filled Gulab Jamuns are pure indulgence. The staff is polite and attentive and the service quick and unobtrusive.
